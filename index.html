GCF Proposal Development

  • Rapidly scan and synthesise peer-reviewed literature, IPCC reports, national adaptation plans, and grey literature to build a robust climate rationale and justify the theory of change.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Summarise the latest evidence on climate change impacts on education in [country], drawing on IPCC reports, national adaptation plans, and recent peer-reviewed studies. Structure by: observed impacts, projected risks, and adaptation gaps."

  • Systematically check the full proposal pack against GCF requirements — completeness, coherence, eligibility criteria, and accreditation standards. Flag gaps before submission.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Review the attached funding proposal against the GCF funding proposal template. For each required section, flag: (1) missing content, (2) internal inconsistencies, and (3) weak justifications. Output as a structured table."

    ★ Claude handles larger proposal packs more reliably due to its longer context window.

  • Answer team and reviewer questions by drawing on the full proposal pack and portfolio precedents — useful for responding to GCF Secretariat queries and peer review comments.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Using the attached proposal, draft a response to the following GCF Secretariat comment: [paste comment]. Keep the response under 300 words and reference specific sections of the proposal."

  • When one section changes, identify all downstream sections affected and draft consistent updates across the concept note, funding proposal, annexes, and safeguards documentation.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "The total budget has changed from USD 8M to USD 6.5M. Review the attached proposal and identify every section that references budget figures or percentages, then suggest revised wording for each."

  • Translate institutional knowledge into structured how-to guides for proposal development, including where and how to use AI at each stage.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Based on the attached process notes and past proposal checklist, draft a step-by-step guide for developing a GCF concept note. Include at each step where AI tools can assist and a suggested prompt."

  • Analyse approved GCF projects to identify patterns in successful proposals — structure, co-financing ratios, indicator frameworks — to strengthen our own submissions.

    How to do it – Copilot or Claude
    Download 5–10 approved GCF project summaries from the GCF website, then ask: "Across these approved proposals, what are the most common co-financing ratios, result indicator types, and safeguard approaches? Summarise patterns in a table."

  • Check that outcomes, outputs, indicators, and assumptions are logically connected, SMART, and aligned with GCF's investment criteria and cross-cutting themes.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Review the attached results framework. For each indicator: (1) assess whether it is SMART, (2) check logical alignment with its parent output/outcome, and (3) flag any gaps in gender or indigenous peoples' cross-cutting coverage."

Project Technical Assistance

  • Distil complex funding proposals into clear, stakeholder-specific briefs — for country teams, government partners, or onboarding documents for new staff.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "From the attached funding proposal, create a 1-page implementation brief for the country team. Focus on: key activities by year, responsible parties, major milestones, and top 3 risks. Use plain language, avoid jargon."

  • Allow team members to query the full project pack in plain language, and surface relevant precedents or lessons from similar projects across the portfolio.

    How to do it – Copilot or Claude
    Upload your full project pack and say: "This is the full documentation for Project X. Answer questions about it as I ask them." Then ask anything — e.g. "What is the indicator target for Output 2.3?" or "What does the proposal say about grievance mechanisms?"

    ★ Claude handles larger document sets more reliably for this use case.

  • Aggregate learning from progress reports, evaluations, and field notes to surface what's working, what isn't, and emerging good practice for adaptive management decisions.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are progress reports from the last 3 reporting periods. Identify: (1) recurring implementation challenges, (2) activities consistently on or ahead of track, and (3) any emerging lessons relevant to our adaptive management plan."

Portfolio Management

  • Consolidate project-level data into portfolio-level summaries for reporting to GCF, SCA leadership, and donors. Reduces manual compilation across spreadsheets.

    How to do it – Copilot
    Export indicator data and narrative updates from each project into a single document or spreadsheet, then ask Copilot: "Summarise portfolio-level progress across these projects, highlighting combined reach figures, common themes in qualitative updates, and any projects flagging risks."

  • Scan progress reports, meeting recordings, and emails to flag recurring risks, implementation bottlenecks, and emerging issues that need escalation.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are the last quarterly progress report, minutes from the last 3 project meetings, and a thread of emails with the country team. Identify any risks or issues mentioned more than once and assess whether any require escalation."

    ★ Copilot can also do this directly in Outlook and Teams if your documents live in M365.

Procurement

  • Screen a long list of expressions of interest against minimum eligibility criteria before full assessment. AI produces a structured summary of each candidate and flags those that clearly don't meet threshold requirements — saving significant reading time up front.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are 20 expressions of interest for [assignment]. Using the eligibility criteria below [paste criteria], assess each applicant and produce a table showing: name, whether they meet each criterion (yes/no/partial), and a one-line rationale. Flag any that clearly fail threshold requirements."

  • Support the full technical assessment of tender proposals by co-developing a scoring framework with sub-criteria, mapping evidence from each proposal against the criteria, scoring proposals with justified rationales, and producing a comparative pros-and-cons summary for final decision-making. Human review and final scoring decisions remain with the team.

    How to do it – Claude (recommended for large proposal sets)
    This is a multi-step process: (1) "Help me develop sub-criteria and a scoring rubric for the following assessment criteria: [paste criteria]." Iterate with your team. (2) "Using the attached proposals and the scoring framework, create an evidence map — for each proposal, summarise the evidence against each criterion." (3) "Using the evidence map, score each proposal per criterion with a brief justification." (4) "Produce a one-page pros and cons summary for each shortlisted proposal to support our final decision."

    ★ Claude Sonnet and Opus are both well-suited for this. Running assessments in both and having colleagues compare responses improves reliability and reduces bias.

  • Analyse consultant or partner budgets for internal consistency, reasonableness of rates, and alignment with the scope of work. Flag unusually high or low line items, check that budget narratives match the figures, and identify missing cost categories or assumptions.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are the Terms of Reference and the consultant's proposed budget. Assess the budget for: (1) whether all ToR deliverables are costed, (2) any line items that appear unusually high or low given the scope, (3) consistency between the budget narrative and the figures, and (4) any costs or assumptions that are missing or unclear."

    ★ For complex multi-partner budgets with many line items, Claude handles the full document more reliably than Copilot.

Programming

  • Strengthen EWS by integrating AI-powered forecasting tools that improve accuracy and lead time of climate hazard predictions, enabling earlier and more targeted community alerts.

    How to do it – Copilot
    This is more about system design than a single prompt. Ask Copilot to help you research AI-powered forecasting tools (e.g. Google DeepMind's weather models, FEWS NET integrations) and draft a scoping brief on how they could enhance your existing EWS in a specific country context.

  • Draft, adapt, and localise behaviour change communication materials, training content, policy briefs, and community resources tailored by audience, reading level, and format.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Rewrite the attached technical brief on heat-resilient schools for a community audience with low literacy. Use short sentences, no jargon, and a question-and-answer format. Aim for a Grade 5 reading level."

  • Translate and culturally adapt project documents, data collection tools, community materials, and reports across languages.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Translate the attached community feedback survey into Bahasa Indonesia. After translating, flag any questions where the phrasing may not be culturally appropriate and suggest alternatives."

  • Develop training programmes, facilitator guides, and learning assessments for climate adaptation, DRR, or M&E capacity building with government and community counterparts.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Design a 2-day training programme for district education officers on integrating climate risk into school planning. Include: learning objectives, session outlines, facilitator notes, and a short post-training assessment."

Project M&E

  • Deploy automated voice or SMS-based surveys for household data collection, post-event monitoring after extreme weather events, and community feedback.

    How to do it – Copilot
    Tools like KoboToolbox, Twilio, or Viamo handle the automation. Use Copilot to design the survey logic first: "Draft a 10-question SMS survey to collect post-flood impact data from households. Include branching logic for households with school-age children."

  • Automate checks for completeness, outliers, inconsistencies, and duplicates, generating flagging reports for enumerator follow-up before analysis.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached is a survey dataset in CSV format. Check for: missing values in key variables, duplicate respondent IDs, out-of-range responses, and logical inconsistencies (e.g. respondent is under 18 but listed as household head). Output a flagging report."

  • Translate survey instruments, KII guides, and FGD protocols while maintaining technical accuracy and cultural appropriateness across languages.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Translate the attached KII guide into Khmer. Preserve the intent of each probing question. Flag any questions that may be sensitive or culturally awkward in a Cambodian context and suggest alternative phrasing."

  • Run descriptive and inferential analysis on quantitative datasets, code qualitative data thematically, and generate annotated outputs ready for reporting.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are 45 KII transcripts from our midline evaluation. Conduct a thematic analysis focused on: barriers to school attendance during extreme weather events, community perceptions of the EWS, and suggestions for improvement. Present findings by theme with illustrative quotes."

    ★ Claude handles large sets of qualitative transcripts more reliably due to its longer context window.

  • Draft results sections and progress narratives structured to meet SCI reporting templates and donor requirements, with placeholders for team validation.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Using the attached indicator data table and project summary, draft the results narrative section for our GCF annual progress report. Follow the SCI reporting template structure. Where data is missing, insert a [PLACEHOLDER – verify with country team] note."

  • Generate charts, maps, dashboards, and infographics for different audiences — from technical log frame progress to community-facing impact stories.

    How to do it – Copilot
    Share your indicator data and ask Copilot: "Suggest the 5 most impactful ways to visualise this results data for a donor report. For each, describe the chart type, what it shows, and what story it tells." Then build them directly in Excel or Power BI within M365.

Climate & Education
For review by C&E team

  • Scan national adaptation plans and education budget documents to assess how well education is integrated and generate evidence-based recommendations for government counterparts.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached is [country]'s National Adaptation Plan and the most recent education sector budget. Assess: (1) whether education is explicitly mentioned in the NAP, (2) whether adaptation measures are costed and funded in the education budget, and (3) the top 3 gaps or recommendations."

  • Draft advocacy briefs, opinion pieces, talking points, and campaign content. Adapt a single piece for multiple audiences — from a technical policy brief to social media or ministerial talking points.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Using the attached evidence brief, produce three versions of the key message on climate finance for education: (1) a 2-page policy brief for a ministry audience, (2) a set of 5 talking points for a ministerial meeting, and (3) three LinkedIn posts."

  • Review, summarise, tag, and categorise new submissions to climateandeducation.org to keep the library useful and searchable as content volume grows.

    Example prompt – Copilot
    "Attached is a new resource submitted for the library. Provide: (1) a 3-sentence summary, (2) suggested tags from this list [paste tag list], (3) geographic focus, (4) recommended content type category, and (5) target audience."

  • Rapidly synthesise country-specific climate, education, and policy data to support TA missions, proposal development, or briefings for government officials and ministry counterparts.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Prepare a country brief for [country] covering: (1) top 3 climate hazards affecting education, (2) current state of climate education policy, (3) key government stakeholders in education and climate, and (4) any existing SCI or partner programming. Use publicly available sources."

  • Review consultant reports against the original terms of reference, checking completeness, logical coherence, methodological rigour, and quality of recommendations. Draft structured feedback.

    Example prompt – Copilot or Claude
    "Attached are the Terms of Reference and the consultant's draft report. Assess: (1) whether all deliverables have been addressed, (2) methodological rigour, (3) whether recommendations are evidence-based and actionable. Draft feedback as a structured memo to the consultant."
